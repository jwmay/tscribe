# Tscribe — How to Install & Use

Tscribe turns a video or audio recording into a text transcript with timestamps.
**Everything happens on your own Mac — nothing is ever uploaded to the internet.**

---

## Installing (one time)

1. Double-click **Tscribe.dmg**.
2. In the window that opens, **drag the Tscribe icon onto the Applications folder**.
3. Open your **Applications** folder and find **Tscribe**.
4. The first time only: **right-click** (or Control-click) Tscribe → choose **Open** → click **Open** again in the box that appears.
   - You only have to do this once. After that, open it normally.
   - This step exists because the app isn't sold through Apple's store — it's yours, made just for you.

---

## Transcribing a recording

1. Open **Tscribe**.
2. **Drag a video or audio file onto the window** (or click **Choose File…**).
3. Wait while it says *Preparing audio…* then *Transcribing…* with a percentage.
   - The first run may take a little longer as it warms up. Long recordings take longer.
4. When it's done, you'll see your recording on the left and the **transcript on the right**.

## Reading & checking the transcript

- **Click any word** to jump the video/audio to exactly that moment and play it — great for verifying what was said.
- The transcript **highlights the current word** as it plays.
- **Word colors** show confidence:
  - normal = confident
  - **orange** = a little uncertain
  - **red / underlined** = low confidence — worth double-checking against the audio
- Hover a colored word to see its confidence.

## Fixing mistakes

- Click the **Edit** button (top right) to correct any text directly. Click it again to stop editing.

## Saving / exporting

Click **Export** (top right) and choose a format:
- **Word document (.docx)** — opens straight into Microsoft Word (timestamps in bold)
- **PDF document (.pdf)** — a clean, paginated, print-ready transcript
- **Plain text** or **Text with timestamps** — for pasting into documents
- **Rich Text (.rtf)** — another Word/Pages-friendly option
- **Subtitles (.srt / .vtt)** — for captioning video

## Coming back later

Tscribe **automatically saves** every transcription, including your edits. The next time
you open the app, your **Recent transcripts** are listed on the start screen — click one
to jump straight back into reviewing, editing, and exporting, with the video synced. No
need to transcribe again. (Saved transcripts live in your **Documents › Tscribe** folder.)

If you've moved the original recording, the transcript still opens — click **Locate
Recording…** to reconnect it for playback.

## Options (on the start screen)

- **Reduce false text in silence** — turn this on for recordings with long pauses (like hearings or calls). It helps stop the app from "inventing" words during quiet stretches.
- **Auto-detect language** — leave off for English; turn on if the recording is in another language.

---

## Important

This is a **machine-generated draft**, not a certified or verbatim transcript. It can make
mistakes — especially with unclear audio, people talking over each other, or names. **Always
verify against the original recording** before relying on it for anything important.

Your recordings and transcripts never leave this Mac.
